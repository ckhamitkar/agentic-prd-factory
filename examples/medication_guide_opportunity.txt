Opportunity: Plain-Language Medication Information Assistant

Many people leave the pharmacy holding a bottle and a folded sheet of small-print
instructions they cannot fully parse. The label says "take one tablet by mouth twice
daily" but rarely explains what the drug is for in plain terms, what the common
warnings mean, or what questions to bring back to a pharmacist. Confusion here leads
to missed doses, doubled doses, and unnecessary calls to already-busy clinics.

The idea is an educational assistant that takes the text of a medication label or
instruction sheet and re-explains it in plain, everyday language: what the instruction
is asking the person to do, what common terms mean, and what warnings to be aware of.
It is strictly educational. It does not diagnose, screen, assess, or treat anything,
and it does not give medical advice. Every response reminds the user to confirm
anything important with a licensed pharmacist or doctor.

Target users are everyday patients and the family members or helpers who manage
medications for them. The product should be simple, accessible to people with a wide
range of literacy and languages, and privacy-preserving - it should ask for as little
personal information as possible.

Open questions for the factory to pressure-test: What is the simplest delivery surface
that reaches real users? What is a sustainable way to fund it without compromising the
privacy or educational-only positioning? Where are the regulatory lines, and how does
the product stay clearly on the educational side of them? Who, if anyone, is a willing
buyer or sponsor, and what would they be buying?
